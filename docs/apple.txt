Apple is a multinational technology company founded in 1976 by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne. The company initially focused on personal computers before expanding into consumer electronics, software, and digital services.

Apple is best known for the iPhone, one of the most successful consumer products in history. The iPhone transformed the smartphone industry and remains one of the company's primary revenue sources.

The company develops a wide range of hardware products including the iPad, MacBook, iMac, Apple Watch, AirPods, and Apple TV. These devices are designed to work seamlessly together within Apple's ecosystem.

Apple develops operating systems including iOS, macOS, iPadOS, watchOS, and tvOS. These operating systems power Apple's hardware products and provide a consistent user experience.

The company operates the App Store, which allows developers to distribute applications to Apple device users worldwide. The App Store contains millions of applications across various categories.

Apple provides digital services such as iCloud, Apple Music, Apple TV+, Apple Arcade, Apple News+, and Apple Pay. These services have become an increasingly important part of Apple's business.

Apple is known for emphasizing privacy and security. The company frequently highlights its commitment to protecting user data through encryption, secure hardware design, and privacy-focused software features.

The company designs its own custom silicon processors known as Apple Silicon. Chips such as the M1, M2, M3, and later generations significantly improved performance and power efficiency in Apple devices.

Apple invests in artificial intelligence, machine learning, augmented reality, and advanced hardware technologies. Many AI-powered features are integrated into Siri, photography systems, accessibility tools, and productivity applications.

Apple's headquarters is Apple Park, located in Cupertino, California. Apple Park is known for its distinctive circular design and serves as the company's main corporate campus.

Apple employs thousands of engineers, designers, researchers, and business professionals. The company continues to influence technology, consumer electronics, software development, and digital services worldwide.
